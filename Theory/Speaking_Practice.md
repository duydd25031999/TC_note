## Pronunciation: Ending Sounds

### 1\. The Past Tense "-ed" Endings

There are **three** ways to pronounce "-ed". The rule depends on the **final sound** of the verb before you add "-ed".

#### **Rule A: The "/Éªd/" Sound (The Extra Syllable)**

* **Architectural Examples:**

#### **Rule C: The "/d/" Sound (Deep & Hummed)**

* **When to use:** When the verb ends in a **voiced** sound (all vowels and other consonants like l, n, v, b, g, m, z).  
* **How it sounds:** Heavy and low. **Do not** add an extra syllable.  
* **Architectural Examples:**

### 2\. The Plural & Third Person "-s/-es" Endings

Similar to "-ed", there are three ways to pronounce the "s" at the end of words.

#### **Rule A: The "/Éªz/" Sound (The Extra Syllable)**

* **When to use:** When the word ends in a "hissing" sound: **s, z, sh, ch, ge/dge** (j sound).
